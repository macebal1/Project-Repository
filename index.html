About Me - Mariella Acebal

Hello! My name is Mariella Acebal and I am 20 years old. The purpose of telling you my story is to tell you a bit about myself and my background as well as how I got so passionate about pursuing a healthy lifestyle. I hope my workouts and recipes serve as inspiration and motivation to start taking care of your body and health. I was born and raised in the Dominican Republic, and then I moved to Boston, Massachusetts to pursue my undergraduate degree at Babson College. Since high school, I battled a lot with my weight and with the way I felt with my body. After trying all types of fad diets, no matter how much exercise I did, I never seemed to shed the weight I wanted off. I was then diagnosed with hypothyroidism and prescribed some pills that would regulate my body hormones. In my ideal world, this was the recipe to shed all the weight I had previously wanted to. Little did I know that this was not the solution. Transitioning into college and living abroad for the first time was very stressful and my body started this rollercoaster weight loss and weight gain process. Due to this, I began all these fad diets once again and changed them every week because I never saw the results I wanted. Fast forward three years, and I am now where I wanted to be years ago and in the best mental and physical condition I have ever been. I have learned a lot throughout this long journey. Most importantly, that it takes time, effort, and consistency to achieve your goals. All the extreme fad diets I was doing, such as living on protein only for 20 days or even a caloric deficit of about 500 calories a day, were definitely not the right way to lose weight mentally and to perform well physically. About three months ago, amid the Coronavirus pandemic that I had to travel back home, my brother inspired me to start changing my mindset. He made me see what I considered “dieting” a lifestyle. Once I accepted this, my transformation has been tremendous. Mentally, I am more aware of how my body feels and less worried about the number on the scale. Physically, I am eating the foods that allow me to perform with the best energy during my long runs and workouts, and my body looks leaner every day. With this I get to my main point: our body needs us to be patient, and our minds can be our worst enemy at times. We have to learn to control our minds and to love our bodies. It took me too much time to realize this, and I just hope that my story helps you realize this sooner. In this blog I will be posting some of the workouts me and my brother do together, as well as some of the recipes we have created at home and love. We hope you enjoy!

About Me - Alfredo Acebal

I aspired to be a pro baseball player, and I trained all my high school to achieve my optimal physical condition. Hello! My name is Alfredo Acebal, and this is my story… Since my teenage years, in which I went to finish my high school degree abroad at IMG Academies in Bradenton, Florida, I have been concerned with my health and fitness. Becoming a baseball player involved healthy nutrition, intense training, and a balanced mindset. My day consisted of baseball, training, eating, studying, and sleeping. However, as the years passed I became more involved in the family business and training and baseball were not necessarily the top priority. Finding time to fit an 8-5 job while playing and training was too complicated. Notwithstanding that, my dream of becoming pro seemed farther and farther every day. This being said, I went abroad again to Boston, Massachusetts to pursue my undergraduate career at Suffolk University. At the time, this is what I thought: “I have spent so much of my time and energy into training, and I did not make it, so this lifestyle of mine is over”. My days now consisted of eating unhealthy and watching television. It was a shocking transformation for me and for those who surrounded me as well. Six years passed, and I had not stepped foot on a gym or on a baseball field. Physically, I looked unhealthy; mentally, I was not happy with my habits and who I had let myself become. I was handling too much stress, but I was not coping with it in a healthy way. Now, at the age of 26, I am the best version of myself. About a year ago, I reintroduced myself in the gym, and about 4 months ago I started what would be the best lifestyle I could follow. It all started by acknowledging the connotation of the word “diet” and making sure I said “lifestyle” every time I thought of this. I have to recognize, however, that the process has not been easy. Getting back on track to becoming the best version of myself physically and mentally has been a long journey involving all my commitment, dedication, and constancy. And that has been my biggest lesson through this all: there will be hard days, but slow and steady wins the race. What my sister and I have created here in terms of content, we hope will inspire you and motivate you to start become your best version yet, and to make sure it lasts! We hope you enjoy!
