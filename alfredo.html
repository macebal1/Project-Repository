About Me - Alfredo Acebal

I aspired to be a pro baseball player, and I trained all my high school to achieve my optimal physical condition. Hello! My name is Alfredo Acebal, and this is my story…

Since my teenage years, in which I went to finish my high school degree abroad at IMG Academies in Bradenton, Florida, I have been concerned with my health and fitness. Becoming a baseball player involved healthy nutrition, intense training, and a balanced mindset. My day consisted of baseball, training, eating, studying, and sleeping. However, as the years passed I became more involved in the family business and training and baseball were not necessarily the top priority. Finding time to fit an 8-5 job while playing and training was too complicated. Notwithstanding that, my dream of becoming pro seemed farther and farther every day.

This being said, I went abroad again to Boston, Massachusetts to pursue my undergraduate career at Suffolk University. At the time, this is what I thought: “I have spent so much of my time and energy into training, and I did not make it, so this lifestyle of mine is over”. My days now consisted of eating unhealthy and watching television. It was a shocking transformation for me and for those who surrounded me as well. Six years passed, and I had not stepped foot on a gym or on a baseball field. Physically, I looked unhealthy; mentally, I was not happy with my habits and who I had let myself become. I was handling too much stress, but I was not coping with it in a healthy way.

Now, at the age of 26, I am the best version of myself. About a year ago, I reintroduced myself in the gym, and about 4 months ago I started what would be the best lifestyle I could follow. It all started by acknowledging the connotation of the word “diet” and making sure I said “lifestyle” every time I thought of this. I have to recognize, however, that the process has not been easy. Getting back on track to becoming the best version of myself physically and mentally has been a long journey involving all my commitment, dedication, and constancy. And that has been my biggest lesson through this all: there will be hard days, but slow and steady wins the race.

What my sister and I have created here in terms of content, we hope will inspire you and motivate you to start become your best version yet, and to make sure it lasts! We hope you enjoy!
